## 1. The Challenge

The core question for legacy publishers is no longer "How do I rank #1?" but "How do I become the **grounded source** for AI Overviews?". The Spruce Pets provides a masterclass in this transition.

## 2. Methodology: The Reverse Engineering Process

To analyze this success, I performed a technical audit of the page's structured data (JSON-LD) to identify hidden "Trust Signals" that Google's AI prefers:

- **Schema Extraction**: Isolated the `application/ld+json` blocks.
- **Entity Mapping**: Identified how the site connects Content (`Article`) to Expertise (`Person`) and Authority (`MedicalWebPage`).
- **Correlation**: Looked at how AI Overviews extract steps even without explicit `HowTo` schema.

## 3. Key Findings: The "Why" Behind the Ranking

### A. The "Reviewer" Moat (EEAT) [.text-h3]
Spruce Pets uses a `reviewedBy` field to create a layer of verification that AI models crave.

- **The Data**: The schema points to Nick Saint-Erne, a Doctor of Veterinary Medicine (DVM).
- **SGO Impact**: Google’s AI avoids giving harmful medical advice. By linking to a verified DVM, the content passes the "Safety Filter," making it eligible for the top AI Overview slot.

### B. Semantic Contextualization [.text-h3]
The content is dual-typed as `Article` AND `MedicalWebPage`.

- **The Data**: `@type: ["Article", "MedicalWebPage"]`.
- **SGO Impact**: This tells the AI precisely which "knowledge bucket" to place the information in, ensuring it shows up for high-intent queries like "how to treat fish fungus."

### C. Factual Grounding [.text-h3]
The logic isn't just "Trust us," it's "Trust the source."

- **The Data**: Inclusion of a `citation` field linked to the University of Florida (EDU).
- **SGO Impact**: AI models (like Gemini) value "Grounding." Providing citable sources in the code helps the AI verify the facts before presenting them to the user.
